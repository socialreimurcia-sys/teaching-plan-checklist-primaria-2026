## 1. Grading Criteria

In our English classes, evaluation is understood as a key element to support students' learning and development. Assessment is not merely a final judgement — it is a continuous, transparent process designed to help students understand their own progress, identify areas for improvement, and ensure that teaching responds to their individual needs.

The foundation of our assessment lies in the Specific Competencies (SPCs) established by Real Decree 157/2022 of 1 March, which sets out the basic curriculum for Primary Education under LOMLOE, and its regional implementation through Decree 209/2022 of 15 September, issued by the education administration for the Región de Murcia. For the area of Foreign Language (English) in Year 5 Primary Education (Third Cycle), these competencies describe what students should progressively be able to do across six key areas of language use.

Each Specific Competency is made measurable through its associated Assessment Criteria (ACs). Each AC describes in observable terms what a student should demonstrate to show competency at the end of the third cycle. Every AC carries a pre-defined percentage weight reflecting its curricular importance (see chart in Section 2 of this document). The final grade is based on how well students meet these weighted Assessment Criteria across all the teaching units of the year.

### 1.1. Using Instruments as Tools

To gather evidence of how students are performing against the Assessment Criteria, we use a variety of activities and instruments throughout our teaching units. Their use is organised into two main categories: **formative instruments**, used primarily to provide timely feedback to both students and teachers — identifying strengths, pinpointing difficulties and guiding teaching during the learning process without carrying heavy weight in the final grade — and **summative instruments**, used towards the end of a unit or learning sequence to measure how well students have consolidated their understanding and met the Assessment Criteria, contributing directly to the final grade.

Among the formative instruments we regularly use, **EdPuzzle (EdP)** allows us to assign interactive video activities that check listening comprehension in real time, while **Plickers (PLK)** provides quick digital polls to gauge whole-class understanding at a glance. **Exit Tickets (ExT)** and **Entry Tickets (EnT)** serve as brief written or oral checks at the close or opening of sessions, activating prior knowledge and identifying gaps before they consolidate. **Direct Observation through the Teacher Diary (DO/TD)** runs throughout every session, capturing qualitative data on participation, communicative behaviour and group interaction.

On the summative side, **Oral Assignments (O.A)** and **Written Assignments (W.A)** provide structured opportunities for students to demonstrate productive competence in speaking and writing respectively, assessed through **Rubrics (RbK)** that ensure consistent and transparent scoring. The **Portfolio** collects curated work samples over time, evidencing growth across multiple ACs, while **Projects (PJ)** integrate competencies holistically within a Learning Situation (Situación de Aprendizaje). **Quizzes (Qz)** consolidate specific ACs at the close of a unit, and **Role Plays (RLP)** and **Cued Dialogues (CD)** assess interactive communicative skills in controlled but realistic contexts.

Two instruments specifically support the development of plurilingual and metacognitive competence: the **Learning Diary / European Language Portfolio (LD/ELP)**, which encourages students to record and reflect on their own progress and difficulties over time, and the **Self-Assessment Quiz (QzSe)**, a student-led tool that builds self-regulation skills. Finally, **Google Classroom Tasks (Google CL)** and **Jigsaw Reading activities (JgswR)** extend assessment into digital and cooperative environments, collecting evidence of mediation, reading and written production skills. A full description of all instruments and their abbreviations is provided in Section 2 of this document.

### 1.2. Calculating Grades: A Step-by-Step Process

The academic year is structured into 3 terms, with 4 teaching units per term, totalling 12 units. Grade calculation follows a clear, three-level sequential process.

**Step 1 — Unit grade.**
Within each teaching unit (approximately 10 sessions), students engage in learning activities assessed against the relevant ACs using both formative and summative instruments. At the end of the unit, all evidence is compiled into a unit score reflecting overall achievement across the evaluated ACs, proportional to their predefined weights.

**Step 2 — Term grade.**
At the end of each term, the term grade is calculated by averaging the scores of the four units: (Unit 1 + Unit 2 + Unit 3 + Unit 4) / 4 = Term Score. This numerical score is then converted into the official qualitative categories for Primary Education.

**Step 3 — Final yearly grade.**
The final yearly grade applies a progressive weighting to the three term grades, recognising that competencies increase in complexity throughout the year. The formula is: **Final Score = [(Term 1 × 1) + (Term 2 × 2) + (Term 3 × 3)] / 6**. This final score is then converted into the student's official grade for the year.

#### Numerical Score to Official Grade Conversion

| Score | Grade | Category |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 0 – 4.9 | INS | Insuficiente |
| 5.0 – 5.9 | SU | Suficiente |
| 6.0 – 6.9 | BI | Bien |
| 7.0 – 7.9 | NOT | Notable |
| 8.0 – 9.9 | SOB | Sobresaliente |

#### Term Weighting System

| Term | Multiplier | Units | Contribution to Final Grade |
|------|-----------|-------|----------------------------|
| Term 1 | × 1 | Units 1–4 | ≈ 16.7% |
| Term 2 | × 2 | Units 5–8 | ≈ 33.3% |
| Term 3 | × 3 | Units 9–12 | ≈ 50.0% |
| **Formula** | | | **Final Score = [ (T1 × 1) + (T2 × 2) + (T3 × 3) ] / 6** |

This structured approach ensures that evaluation is a meaningful, transparent and equitable process aligned with the learning goals of Decree 209/2022. It supports the progressive development of communicative competence, reflects growth over time, and provides students and families with a clear and honest picture of achievement.

## 2. Assessment Criteria, Weights & Instruments — Year 5 EP

The following table presents the complete Assessment Criteria (ACs) for Year 5 Primary Education, derived from the Specific Competencies defined in Real Decree 157/2022 and Decree 209/2022 (Murcia, Third Cycle). For each AC, the table specifies its global weight in the yearly grade and the instruments used to assess it. This table is the primary reference when elaborating individual unit assessment charts.

| SPC & Weight | Assessment Criteria (AC) | AC Wt. (%) | Formative Instruments | Summative Instruments |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SPC (1) Receptive (20%)** | Identify and understand the overall meaning and specific information from simple oral and written texts on familiar topics, using basic comprehension strategies and available supports. | AC 1.1 — 10% | EdP, ExT, Plickers | ExT (from EdP), Qz (EnT) |
| **SPC (1) Receptive (20%)** | Use basic strategies (visual context, prior knowledge, key words) to predict, infer meaning and check comprehension of oral and written texts. | AC 1.2 — 10% | Plickers, EdP, ExT | ExT, EnT, Qz |
| **SPC (2) Productive (15%)** | Orally produce short, simple, structured and comprehensible texts on familiar everyday topics, using basic phonological resources and adapting register to the situation. | AC 2.1 — 5% | DO (Observed), ExT | Project Pres. (RbK), O.A |
| **SPC (2) Productive (15%)** | Write and share simple written texts on familiar topics, following basic genre conventions and using cohesion resources with guidance. | AC 2.2 — 5% | ExT, EnT, DO (TD) | Portfolio, W.A, (RbK) |
| **SPC (2) Productive (15%)** | Organise and apply strategies to plan, monitor and review oral and written production, reflecting on the communicative process and applying basic self-correction techniques. | AC 2.3 — 5% | ExT, EnT, DO (TD) | Portfolio, W.A, (RbK) *alongside AC 2.2* |
| **SPC (3) Interaction (15%)** | Participate in simple, short face-to-face interactions on predictable everyday topics, using basic strategies to initiate, maintain and close communication. | AC 3.1 — 10% | RLP, Cued Dialogue, Drama, InfoGap | O.A (RbK), (Ex) |
| **SPC (3) Interaction (15%)** | Use simple cooperative strategies (turn-taking, basic repair, checking understanding) to participate effectively in group tasks and classroom activities. | AC 3.2 — 5% | RLP, Cued Dialogue, Drama | O.A (RbK) *alongside AC 3.1* |
| **SPC (4) Mediation (20%)** | Explain, in a simple way, short texts, concepts and everyday situations to peers, using available resources and supports to bridge linguistic or cultural gaps. | AC 4.1 — 10% | DO (TD), JgswR, ExT, EnT | O.A (Ex)(RbK), W.A (Google CL) |
| **SPC (4) Mediation (20%)** | Apply basic strategies to simplify and reformulate texts, supporting others through paraphrase, examples and non-verbal resources. | AC 4.2 — 10% | DO (TD), JgswR, ExT, EnT | O.A (Ex)(RbK), W.A (Google CL) *alongside AC 4.1* |

## 3. Legislative and Curricular Framework

This document is grounded in the following legislative and curricular framework applicable to Primary Education in the Región de Murcia:

- **Ley Orgánica 3/2020, de 29 de diciembre (LOMLOE)**, por la que se modifica la Ley Orgánica 2/2006, de Educación.
- **Real Decreto 157/2022, de 1 de marzo**, por el que se establecen la ordenación y las enseñanzas mínimas de la Educación Primaria.
- **Decreto 209/2022, de 15 de septiembre**, por el que se establece la ordenación y el currículo de la etapa de Educación Primaria en la Comunidad Autónoma de la Región de Murcia (BORM núm. 267, 18 de noviembre de 2022).
- **Marco Común Europeo de Referencia para las Lenguas (MCER)** — nivel orientativo A2.1 para el alumnado de 5º EP.

Assessment Criteria are drawn from Anexo III of Decreto 209/2022 (Tercer Ciclo, Lengua Extranjera — Inglés) and are aligned with the competency descriptors established in the Exit Profile (Perfil de Salida) for Primary Education.
